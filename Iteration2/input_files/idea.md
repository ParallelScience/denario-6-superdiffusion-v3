**Title**: Velocity PDF Scaling and Anomalous Transport in 3D Quenched Vortex Filament Fields

**Description**: This study characterizes 3D tracer advection by analyzing the empirical velocity PDF and MSD scaling across filament densities $N \in \{5, 10, 20, 40\}$. Leveraging noise-free `x_true` trajectories, we directly compute the velocity distribution tails to test for convergence toward the theoretical Holtsmark distribution ($\alpha_{stable}=1.5$) predicted by the $1/r^2$ Biot-Savart kernel. We quantify the anomalous diffusion exponent $\alpha(N)$ via power-law fitting of the 3D MSD, using the $\beta=1.5$ Lévy walk ground truth as a baseline to define the expected theoretical transport regime. By comparing the VACF decay of the filament system against the isotropic Lévy walk, we isolate the impact of quenched spatial disorder on velocity memory versus purely stochastic jumps. This analysis provides a rigorous validation of the 3D-specific transport limit, explicitly contrasting the observed $\alpha(N)$ convergence with the Cauchy-dominated results of 2D point-vortex systems.
