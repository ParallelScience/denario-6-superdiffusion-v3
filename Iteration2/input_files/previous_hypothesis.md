The persistent superdiffusive bias ($\alpha > 1.5$) in 3D quenched vortex filament systems arises because the tracer trajectories are dominated by "ballistic corridors" near individual filaments, which are not captured by the isotropic Lévy walk model. I hypothesize that the transport process is a **superposition of two distinct regimes**: (1) a "trapping" regime in rotation-dominated regions (identified by the Okubo-Weiss criterion) and (2) a "ballistic flight" regime along the strain-dominated manifolds of the filaments. 

To test this, I propose a **Conditional Velocity Autocorrelation Function (CVACF)** analysis: decompose the VACF based on the local Okubo-Weiss (OW) parameter. By calculating the VACF separately for tracers in rotation-dominated ($OW < 0$) versus strain-dominated ($OW > 0$) regions, we can quantify the contribution of each to the anomalous exponent. I hypothesize that the strain-dominated regions are responsible for the persistent velocity correlations that prevent the system from reaching the theoretical Holtsmark $\alpha=1.5$ limit. By filtering out these ballistic segments, the remaining "trapping" dynamics should exhibit a convergence toward the predicted $\alpha=1.5$ regime, effectively reconciling the deterministic fluid topology with the stochastic Lévy walk theory.
