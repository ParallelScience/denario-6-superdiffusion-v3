- Justification: The current results provide a high-quality, physically rich dataset that captures the transition from ballistic-dominated transport to a more complex, quenched-disorder regime. The "failure" to match the asymptotic Holtsmark exponent ($\alpha=1.5$) is not a negative result; it is a significant scientific finding that characterizes the finite-time persistence of quenched vortex fields. The proposed changes—specifically the bimodal decomposition of displacement PDFs and the conditional probability analysis of the Okubo-Weiss criterion—directly address the "Publishability Delta" by moving the paper from a descriptive analysis to a mechanistic study of transport regimes. These improvements will satisfy reviewers by providing a rigorous explanation for the observed MSD exponents and clarifying the fundamental difference between the quenched vortex system and renewal-based Lévy walks. The ROI is high because the data is already generated; the effort is purely analytical and will significantly strengthen the paper's theoretical contribution.
